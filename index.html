Projects

Finding a Micro RNA Treatment for COVID-19

I created a computer model that automates the identification of miRNA sequences that can inhibit SARS-CoV-2 replication through RNA interference. My computer model uses a combination of web scraping and nucleotide alignments to identify 169 miRNA sequences of significant interest, notably miR-302c. This work was recognzied at the 2021 Regeneron ISEF science fair, the largest science and engineering fair in the world, by winning 4th place in computational biology and bioinformatics.

Using Deep Learning to Solve Spinal Cord Regeneration

I used a Convolutional Neural Network and graph traversal algorithms to predict how networks of transcripton factors interact with each other. Through the development of this model, I identified CHD8 as a potential candidate to stimulate neuron regeneration. Under the supervision of Prof. Kessen Patten at the INRS Armand-Frappier, we discovered that CHD8 mRNA expression levels increased by 40% in zebrafish undergoing spinal regeneration. This work was recognized by winning 1st place in Quebec and 3rd in Canada during the Sanofi Biogenius Challenge, and received a Bronze Medal at the 2019 Canada-Wide Science Fair.

Video to Text Translation for American Sign Language

Our team used Google Cloud to train a deep learning model to translate American Sign Langauge videos to text with >99% accuracy. Our work was awarded 3rd place at HackHarvard 2019, Harvard University's annual hackathon.
